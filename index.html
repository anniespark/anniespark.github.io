Afterschool Data Visualization Website

Design & Front-End

Visit the Website

Afterschool is a website that uses data from the DELPHI database and Eventbrite API to populate a map of the San Diego area with details about elementary schools and afterschool activities children can do nearby. Our goal is to help parents find things for their child to do after school that benefit their child emotionally, socially and physically.
For this project, I worked with four other people to complete this project in four weeks. My role was the UX/UI Designer, Visual Designer and front-end developer in the team. To bring this website to completion, we went through multiple iterations of mockups until we reached the final prototype and implemented the website.

The Design Process

Brainstorm

We first brainstormed on website ideas by going through personas of different users and their potential needs. Going off of this, we researched different needs in San Diego and looked into Delphi databases to see what kind of data there is for us to use on our website. At the end of all this, we had a target user with specific needs.
Target Users:
Parents with elementary school age children.
Need:
Parents need to an easier way to find beneficial activities for their children to participate in after school. All parents desire to find good places and activities for children, but it still remains hard to find.

Paper Prototypes

We created two different paper prototypes, and after discussion and testing it out, we decided to take elements of both to create one idea.

Low-Fidelity Wireframe

View the Wireframe

I created the wireframe for the website with Axure RP based on our two paper prototypes but with new changes to make a more user-friendly interface.

To give more room for the map, the most essential part of the website, the left bar menu that was in the paper prototype was moved to the bottom of the screen. The user can navigate while still having enough room to view the map.

Since the purpose of our website is significant, this page was added to explain what the website provides and why it matters for children to find better activities to do after school.

Interactive Prototype with Axure

View the Prototype

After going through the wireframe, I designed a high-fidelity interactive prototype in which important design choices were made.

I made an overall visual representation of San Diego separated into different school districts. The idea was to give the user the choice to type in the school name at the top or choose the distrcit.

The map visual is flexible and allows users to select any combination of options. The unique colors for each type of location can be easily understood by users. Users have the ability to interactively choose what kinds of activities they want to see in what distance along with explanations.

After researching on different statistics, I was able to put together a simple representation of what the website's goals are and why after school activities matter.

Implementaton & Final Design

View the Website
For implementation, I mainly worked with HTML & CSS with the Materialize framework to code the splash screen, navigation bar, color edits, and the 'why afterschool activities matter' and 'about' portion of the page.
Splash Screen

The purpose of the splash screen is to ease the users to a more minimalistic design with an important overview of what the website provides before users see a more complex map screen as they proceed to the rest of the website.

Cluster Map

This cluster map shows a visual of how many schools are in an area. When the user clicks on different options in 'Places to Go', the clusters are updated. The 'Events' portion is linked to the Eventbrite API to display kid-friendly events to attend. Users also have the option to find directions from one place to another.

Heat Map

The user can toggle to see the cluster map or this heat map. The heat map provides a simple visual of which areas have more activities to do.

Why It Matters

This portion was changed to make it more readable. The problem is clearly stated and the benefits are shown to the user to bring them to take action.
